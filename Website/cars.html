F1 Cars Over the Years

  • Ferrari SF90 (2020)

    Ferrari's 2020 contender, powered by a turbocharged V6 engine and advanced aerodynamics.

  • Mercedes W11 (2020)

    A dominant car that won both drivers' and constructors' championships in 2020, known for its high-tech innovations.

  • Red Bull Racing RB18 (2022)

    A key contender in the 2022 season, featuring cutting-edge aerodynamics and a new era power unit.

  • McLaren MCL35M (2021)

    The McLaren MCL35M showcased solid performance with a focus on aerodynamics and a revised engine partnership with Mercedes.

  • Aston Martin AMR23 (2023)

    The AMR23 car, led by Sebastian Vettel in 2023, focuses on aerodynamic efficiency and a strong power unit to compete with top teams.

  • Ferrari SF1000 (2020)

    Ferrari's SF1000 was a step towards a more competitive season but struggled with power unit performance, ending in mixed results.

  • Renault RS20 (2020)

    The RS20 was a significant improvement for Renault, thanks to a more powerful engine and improved aerodynamics.

  • AlphaTauri AT02 (2021)

    The AT02 was one of the most consistent mid-field cars in the 2021 season, balancing pace and reliability throughout the year.

Innovative F1 Technology

Formula 1 cars are known for their cutting-edge technology, from hybrid power units to advanced aerodynamics. Each car is designed to be as fast as possible while maintaining safety and reliability.
